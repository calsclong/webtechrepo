FAQ – Questions about the rovers and Mars

Why are rovers sent to Mars?

Rovers are sent to Mars to study its surface, climate, and history. They help scientists determine whether the planet once had conditions suitable for life and gather data for future human missions.

How long does it take to get to Mars?

A trip to Mars typically takes about 6 to 7 months, depending on the alignment of Earth and Mars and the spacecraft’s trajectory.

How do rovers communicate with Earth?

Rovers send signals to satellites orbiting Mars, which then relay the information back to Earth. Because of the distance, communication can take between 5 and 20 minutes one way.

How are rovers powered?

Some rovers, like Sojourner, Spirit, and Opportunity, used solar panels for power. Others, like Curiosity and Perseverance, use nuclear-powered systems that generate electricity from heat.
