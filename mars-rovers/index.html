Sojourner

Mission goals and overview

The Sojourner rover was part of the Mars Pathfinder mission. Its main goals were to test new, low-cost ways of landing on Mars and to explore the planet’s surface. It was designed to study rocks and soil and to prove that a mobile rover could successfully operate on Mars.

Launch and landing details

Mars Pathfinder launched on December 4, 1996, and landed on Mars on July 4, 1997, in Ares Vallis. The mission used an innovative airbag landing system to safely reach the surface.

Major discoveries or accomplishments

Sojourner provided the first close-up analyses of Martian rocks, confirming that some were shaped by water in the past. It successfully demonstrated that rovers could operate on Mars, paving the way for future missions.

Key technical or design features

Sojourner was a small, six-wheeled rover weighing about 23 pounds. It used solar panels for power and could be remotely controlled from Earth. It also featured hazard detection and autonomous navigation capabilities.

Perseverance

Mission goals and overview

The Perseverance rover is part of NASA’s Mars 2020 mission. Its main goals are to search for signs of ancient life, study the planet’s geology and climate, and collect samples that may be returned to Earth in the future. It also helps prepare for future human exploration of Mars.

Launch and landing details

Perseverance launched on July 30, 2020, and landed on February 18, 2021, in Jezero Crater. It used a sky crane landing system, similar to the Curiosity rover, for a precise touchdown.

Major discoveries or accomplishments

Perseverance has collected and stored Martian samples for future return to Earth, discovered evidence of ancient river and delta systems, and deployed the Ingenuity helicopter—the first powered flight on another planet.

Key technical or design features

Perseverance is a large, car-sized rover equipped with advanced scientific instruments, AI-based navigation, and high-resolution cameras. It also carries a drill for sample collection and the Ingenuity helicopter for aerial exploration.
